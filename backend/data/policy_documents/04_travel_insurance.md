# Travel Insurance

## Overview
Travel insurance provides financial protection against medical emergencies, trip cancellations, lost baggage, flight delays, and other unforeseen events during domestic or international travel.

## Key Features
- Medical expense coverage abroad (typically $50,000 to $1,000,000)
- Trip cancellation/interruption coverage
- Lost baggage and personal belongings coverage
- Flight delay compensation (usually after 6-12 hours)
- Personal accident coverage
- Repatriation of remains
- 24x7 emergency assistance hotline

## Types of Travel Insurance

### Domestic Travel Insurance
- Covers medical emergencies within India
- Baggage loss and delay
- Trip cancellation
- Lower premium (typically Rs. 100-500 for short trips)

### International Travel Insurance
- Required for Schengen visa applications (minimum 30,000 Euro coverage)
- Comprehensive medical coverage abroad
- Emergency evacuation and repatriation
- Higher premium (typically Rs. 500-5000 depending on destination and duration)

### Student Travel Insurance
- For students studying abroad
- Coverage includes tuition fees, accommodation
- Medical coverage including mental health
- Typically 1-4 year coverage

## Exclusions
- Pre-existing medical conditions (unless specifically covered)
- Adventure sports (unless extra premium paid)
- Losses due to war, terrorism, nuclear risks
- Self-inflicted injuries
- Losses from illegal activities
- Claims from intoxicated state

## Claim Process
1. Contact emergency assistance number immediately
2. For medical: visit network hospital for cashless treatment
3. For baggage: file PIR (Property Irregularity Report) at airport
4. For trip cancellation: submit cancellation documents
5. Submit all original receipts and proofs within 30 days

## Ideal For
- International travelers (mandatory for visa countries)
- Frequent business travelers
- Students going abroad for studies
- Family vacation travelers
- Senior citizens traveling abroad

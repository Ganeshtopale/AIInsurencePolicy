# Claim Process and FAQ

## General Claim Process

### Step-by-Step Guide
1. Intimate the insurer immediately (within 24 hours for accidents, 30 days for other events)
2. Gather all required documents (policy document, identity proof, incident reports)
3. Fill and submit claim form (online or physical)
4. Insurer appoints a surveyor for assessment (within 48 hours)
5. Surveyor submits report within 30 days
6. Insurer processes claim and communicates decision
7. Payment made within 7-30 days of approval

### Documents Required
- Duly filled claim form
- Original policy document
- Identity proof of claimant/nominee
- For death claims: Death certificate, Medical certificate of cause of death
- For health claims: Medical reports, Hospital bills, Prescriptions
- For motor claims: Driving license, RC book, FIR copy
- For travel claims: Travel itinerary, PIR report, Medical certificates

### Cashless vs Reimbursement
- Cashless: Treatment at network hospital; insurer pays directly to hospital
- Reimbursement: Pay first at non-network hospital; claim later from insurer
- Most health insurers have 4000+ network hospitals across India

### Claim Rejection Reasons
- Non-disclosure of pre-existing conditions at policy purchase
- Claim for excluded events (cosmetic surgery, dental, etc.)
- Claim during waiting period (first 30 days for health policies)
- Policy not in force (premium not paid, policy lapsed)
- Incomplete documents or delayed intimation
- Fraudulent claims

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1: What is the difference between term and whole life insurance?
A: Term insurance covers you for a fixed period (e.g., 20 years) and pays only if you die within that term. Whole life insurance covers you for your entire lifetime and also builds cash value. Term is much more affordable and is recommended for most people.

### Q2: How much life insurance coverage do I need?
A: A common rule is 10-15 times your annual income. You can also calculate: sum of all debts + future expenses (children's education, marriage) + family's annual expenses x 10 - current savings.

### Q3: Can I have multiple health insurance policies?
A: Yes. If you have multiple policies, you can claim from one and then claim the remaining from the other (under the Contribution Clause). This is useful for high-cost treatments.

### Q4: What is a free look period?
A: It's the period (15-30 days) after receiving the policy document during which you can review the policy. If you're not satisfied, you can cancel and get a refund of the premium paid (minus some charges).

### Q5: What happens if I miss a premium payment?
A: You get a grace period of 15-30 days. If paid within this period, the policy continues without any penalty. If not paid, the policy lapses. Some policies have a revival period of 2-5 years to reinstate the policy.

### Q6: Are mental health conditions covered in health insurance?
A: After the Mental Healthcare Act 2017, insurance companies are required to cover mental health conditions on par with physical illnesses. However, specific coverage varies by policy.

### Q7: What is Portability in health insurance?
A: Portability allows you to switch from one health insurer to another without losing the benefits of continuous coverage, including credited no-claim bonus and completed waiting periods.

### Q8: How does GST impact insurance premiums?
A: GST on insurance premiums is 18%. This means if your base premium is Rs. 10,000, you pay Rs. 11,800 including GST.

### Q9: What is the difference between a nominee and a beneficiary?
A: In insurance, these terms are often used interchangeably. The nominee is the person named in the policy who receives the death benefit. For ULIPs and endowment plans, the beneficiary may differ from the nominee.

### Q10: Can NRIs buy insurance in India?
A: Yes, NRIs can buy insurance in India. Term life, health, and ULIP plans are available. However, premiums may be higher for NRIs in certain countries due to higher risk assessment.

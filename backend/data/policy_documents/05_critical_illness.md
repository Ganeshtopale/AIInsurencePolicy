# Critical Illness Insurance

## Overview
Critical illness insurance provides a lump sum payout upon diagnosis of a specified critical illness. Unlike health insurance that covers hospitalization expenses, critical illness insurance pays the full sum assured regardless of actual treatment costs. This money can be used for treatment, loss of income, lifestyle changes, or any other purpose.

## Key Features
- Lump sum payout on diagnosis (not reimbursement-based)
- Coverage for 20-40 critical illnesses
- Premiums are tax deductible under Section 80D
- Can be bought as standalone policy or as rider with term/life insurance
- Payout is in addition to any other insurance coverage

## Commonly Covered Illnesses
- Cancer (all stages)
- Heart attack (first heart attack)
- Stroke
- Kidney failure
- Major organ transplant
- Coronary artery bypass surgery
- Multiple sclerosis
- Paralysis
- Coma
- Deafness
- Blindness
- Loss of speech
- Parkinson's disease
- Alzheimer's disease
- Aortic surgery

## Exclusions
- Pre-existing conditions (first 2-4 years)
- Self-inflicted injuries
- HIV/AIDS
- Participation in hazardous sports
- War and nuclear risks
- Certain cancers (some insurers exclude early-stage or non-invasive cancers)

## Premium Factors
- Age (critical factor - premiums increase significantly with age)
- Smoking status
- Family medical history
- Lifestyle and occupation
- Sum assured (typically Rs. 5 lakhs to Rs. 1 crore)
- Number of illnesses covered

## Waiting Periods
- Initial waiting period: 90 days from policy start
- Specific illnesses may have longer waiting periods (e.g., 6 months for certain cancers)
- Survival period: usually 30 days (must survive 30 days after diagnosis to claim)

## Ideal For
- People with family history of critical illnesses
- Primary breadwinners (income protection)
- Those with insufficient health insurance coverage
- Individuals aged 25-55 (best time to buy)
- Those with high-stress lifestyles

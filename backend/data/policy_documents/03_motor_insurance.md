# Motor Insurance

## Overview
Motor insurance provides coverage for vehicles against accidents, theft, fire, and third-party liabilities. In India, motor insurance is mandatory under the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988. There are two main types: third-party liability (mandatory) and comprehensive coverage.

## Types of Motor Insurance

### Third-Party Insurance
- Covers legal liability for death, injury, or property damage caused to a third party
- Mandatory by law
- Does NOT cover damage to own vehicle
- Lower premium compared to comprehensive

### Comprehensive Insurance
- Covers own vehicle damage and third-party liability
- Protection against: accidents, fire, theft, natural disasters, riots, strikes
- Personal accident cover for owner-driver (sum insured up to Rs. 15 lakhs)
- Optional add-ons available

## Add-on Covers
- Zero Depreciation Cover (Nil Depreciation): Full claim without depreciation deduction
- Engine Protector: Covers engine damage due to water ingression, oil leakage
- Roadside Assistance: Towing, flat tire, fuel assistance
- Return to Invoice: Get full invoice value if vehicle is totalled or stolen
- Consumables Cover: Covers nuts, bolts, oil, coolant costs during repair
- NCB Protection: Protects accumulated no-claim bonus even after a claim
- Key and Lock Replacement: Coverage for lost or damaged keys

## No Claim Bonus (NCB)
- 20% discount for first claim-free year (up to 50% max after 5+ years)
- NCB is transferable if switching insurers
- NCB is valid for 90 days after policy expiry
- NCB is lost if a claim is made during the year

## Premium Factors
- Vehicle IDV (Insured Declared Value): Higher IDV = higher premium
- Cubic capacity (engine CC): Higher CC = higher premium
- Age of vehicle: Older vehicles have lower IDV
- Geographical location: Urban areas have higher premiums
- NCB: Claim-free years reduce premium
- Fuel type: Diesel vehicles may have different rates

## Claim Process
1. Intimate insurer immediately after accident (within 24 hours)
2. Lodge FIR with police (for theft or third-party injury)
3. Take photos/videos of damage
4. Submit vehicle to network garage for cashless repair
5. Submit required documents: driving license, RC, policy copy, FIR
6. Surveyor assesses damage within 48 hours
7. Repair approved and completed

## Ideal For
- All vehicle owners (mandatory by law)
- New car owners (comprehensive recommended)
- Used car buyers (transfer existing NCB)

# Term Life Insurance

## Overview
Term life insurance is the simplest and most affordable form of life insurance. It provides coverage for a fixed period (the "term"), typically 10, 15, 20, 25, or 30 years. If the policyholder dies during the term, the nominees receive the death benefit (sum assured). If the policyholder survives the term, no payout is made.

## Key Features
- Pure protection plan with no maturity benefit
- High sum assured at low premiums
- Premiums remain fixed throughout the term
- Tax benefits under Section 80C (premiums) and Section 10(10D) (death benefit)
- Optional riders: critical illness, accidental death, waiver of premium

## Eligibility
- Minimum age: 18 years
- Maximum age at entry: usually 65 years
- Maximum age at maturity: usually 75-85 years
- Sum assured: typically 10-30 times annual income

## Ideal For
- Young professionals with financial dependents
- Home loan borrowers (coverage equals outstanding loan)
- Parents with young children (education and marriage goals)
- Primary breadwinners of families

## Exclusions
- Suicide within first 12 months (policy is void)
- Death due to participation in hazardous activities
- Death under the influence of drugs or alcohol
- Pre-existing diseases not disclosed at time of purchase

## Claim Process
1. Notify the insurer within 30 days of death
2. Submit death certificate, policy document, identity proofs
3. Nominee submits claim forms
4. Insurer processes and pays within 30 days of document submission
5. In case of investigation, claims settled within 90 days

## Premium Factors
- Age (younger = lower premium)
- Smoking status (smokers pay 30-50% more)
- Occupation (high-risk jobs increase premium)
- Health history (pre-existing conditions increase premium)
- Sum assured and term length (higher coverage = higher premium)

# General Insurance Guide

## Understanding Insurance Basics

### What is Insurance?
Insurance is a contract between an individual (insured) and an insurance company (insurer) where the insurer promises to provide financial protection against specified losses in exchange for regular premium payments. It works on the principle of risk pooling - many people pay premiums, and the collected fund is used to compensate those who experience losses.

### Key Terms
- Premium: The amount paid periodically (monthly, quarterly, half-yearly, yearly) to keep the policy active
- Sum Assured / Coverage Amount: The maximum amount the insurer will pay in case of a claim
- Deductible: The amount the policyholder pays before insurance coverage kicks in
- Waiting Period: The time period after policy start when certain coverages are not available
- Exclusions: Specific conditions or events NOT covered by the policy
- Nominee: The person who receives the insurance payout in case of the policyholder's death
- Rider: An additional benefit that can be added to a base policy for extra premium
- Free Look Period: 15-30 days from policy receipt to review and cancel if not satisfied
- Grace Period: 15-30 days after premium due date to pay without policy lapse
- Surrender Value: The amount returned if policy is cancelled before maturity

### Types of Insurance
1. Life Insurance: Term, Whole Life, Endowment, ULIP, Money Back
2. Health Insurance: Individual, Family Floater, Critical Illness, Senior Citizen, Top-up
3. Motor Insurance: Third-party, Comprehensive
4. Travel Insurance: Domestic, International, Student
5. Home Insurance: Structure, Contents, Both
6. Business Insurance: Liability, Property, Marine, Professional Indemnity

### How Premiums Are Calculated
Insurance companies use actuarial science to calculate premiums based on:
- Risk assessment of the individual (age, health, lifestyle, occupation)
- Historical claims data for similar profiles
- Coverage amount and policy term
- Investment returns on premium pool
- Operating expenses and profit margin
- Regulatory requirements (IRDAI guidelines)

### Claim Settlement Ratio (CSR)
CSR is the percentage of claims an insurer has settled against total claims received. A higher CSR (90%+) indicates better claim settlement track record. Always check CSR when choosing an insurer.

### IRDAI - The Regulator
The Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India (IRDAI) regulates the insurance industry in India. It ensures fair practices, protects policyholder interests, and sets guidelines for premiums, claims, and insurer solvency.

### Tax Benefits
- Section 80C: Life insurance premiums up to Rs. 1.5 lakhs per year
- Section 80D: Health insurance premiums up to Rs. 25,000 (Rs. 50,000 for senior citizens)
- Section 10(10D): Death benefit and maturity proceeds (subject to conditions)
- Section 80CCD(1B): Additional Rs. 50,000 for NPS
